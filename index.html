Pregnancy Cravings





What are Pregnancy Cravings?

Pregnancy cravings are irresistable urges to eat a woman may get while she is pregnant. The cravings can range from something sweet like chocolate or candy to stuff like meat or junk food. These pregnancy cravings can be triggered by hormonal changes in the body that usually start around the 1st trimester, and end around the 4th or 5th week.

Pregnancy cravings happen to about 90% of women during their pregnancy.

It is completely normal for a pregnant woman to have these intense cravings, unless they start craving strange foods or things that aren't food.




Why do I get Pregnancy Cravings?

While there is no exact reason, there are two main theories as to why women get them. One theory suggests it is because of the increase in hormones that can highten the sense of smell and taste. The increase of these hormones can make the woman feel more hungry than usual.

The other theory suggests that it is the bodys way to signal a change in your nutrional needs.

Some other reaons as to why a pregnant woman gets pregnancy cravings can be drawn back to a lack of vitamins, iron, or protein in the body.




How do I stop them?

There are a few ways to hold off the cravings, such as snacking on healthy foods, drink a lot of water, and exercise.

As tempting as it may be to eat what your body is telling you to eat, dont give in. Doing this will make it worse and therefore have the cravings continue.




What is Pica?

Pica is a mental health condition where a person eats non food items. For the most part, Pica is harmless. It only becomes harmful if you eat something dangerous or bad for you.

Pica is most common in young children, pregnant women, and people with other mental health disorders. It can be caused by an immense amount of stress/anxiety, pre-existing medical conditions, or nutrional deficiencies.

Some common stuff people with Pica eat are ash, chalk, dirt, charcoal, hair, feces, and other random stuff.




Placenta Eating

A common thing for women to do is to eat their placenta, especially of their cravings continue after they give birth. While not proven, there are many benefits of doing this such as an improvement in mood, energy, milk supply, and provide important nutrients and vitamins.

Eating your own placenta can even prevent postpartum depression, something many women struggle with after giving birth.

FAQ's

What if they do not stop after the 5th week?

If the Pregnancy cravings do not stop after the 5th week, contact your healthcare provider.


What are the strangest cravings?

Some of the strangest pregnancy cravings include ice, raw meat, pickles, and ice cream.


Are there any psychological effects from pregnancy cravings?

As of now, there are no known psycohologial effects from pregnancy cravings. However, it has been found in a recent study that giving into your cravings and eating recuces pregnancy-related stress.

Not eating duirng your cravings can be harmful to you and your baby because it is always a good idea to stay fed during a pregnancy.


How do pregnancy cravings differ from non-pregnancy cravings?

There is not much of a difference betwewen the two types of cravings, but pregnancy cravings may be a little stranger or than normal crvaings. For example you may be craving foods you do not particularly like or just strange foods.

Pregnancy cravings are stronger though, and feel more way intense than normal cravings.
