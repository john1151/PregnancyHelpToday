Placenta Eating





What is a placenta?

A placenta is an organ that develops in the womans uterus during pregnancy. It provides nutrients and oxygen to the fetus. The placenta also removes waste from the fetus's blood, and filters out any bacteria going to the fetus.

The placenta attatches to the wall of the uterus, and connects the umbilical cord to the baby.

While being an organ, some women actually eat their own placenta after giving birth.




Why do women eat their placenta?

Placentophagia is the ingestion of the placenta after giving birth.

Women usually eat their own placenta to help prevent postpartum depression. Postpartum depression is feeling depressed after giving birth. Many women struggle with it after childbirth, so preventing it is a big deal.

Eating it also helps improve mood, energy, milk supply, and provides important vitamins and nutrients such as iron.

In a lot of cases, eating your placenta has also stopped pregnancy cravings. This could be good for any women who still have strong pregnancy cravings even after giving birth




Why shouldn't I eat it?

In some cases, the placenta may be infected with bacteria that is bad for you. This is because a placenta acts like a filter, making sure the baby doesn't get any bad bacteria and stuff like that.

Furthermore, it is not confirmed that eating a placenta actually gives any benefits mentioned above.




Side effects

One side effect of eating your placenta is the ability to prevent postpartum depression, as mentioned above. While not confirmed, many women say this works and makes them feel better. 85% of women who ate their placenta said they did not develop postpartum depression.

Another side effect is that eating it reduces or gets rid of all pregancy cravings. This is important if you are still struggling with pregnancy cravings even after giving birth. It can even cure Pica, if you may have developed that during pregnancy. 85% of women that had Pica said eating their placenta cured their Pica.
